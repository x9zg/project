Private Sector Flood Response

Joppatowne Flood Scenario Guide

Scenario: A devastating flood has hit Joppatowne. Over 100 homes and all businesses have been destroyed.

THIRA Steps

  1. Identify Threats and Hazards: Flooding impacts infrastructure, supply chains, workforce.
  2. Give Context: Assess access, logistics, and employee safety.
  3. Establish Capability Targets: Restore 50% operations in 72 hrs; protect employees.
  4. Apply Results: Update plans, coordinate with officials, share best practices.

SPR Steps

  1. Assess Current Capabilities: Review continuity, cybersecurity, emergency contact systems.
  2. Identify Capability Gaps: Lack of insurance, backup power, remote access.
  3. Address Gaps: Train staff, invest in resilience, partner with emergency services.

Private Sector Response Group & Roles

  • Local Business Owners: Damage assessment, reopening, employee support.
  • Corporate Chains: Provide food, fuel, medicine.
  • Utilities & Telecom: Restore power, water, communications.
  • Insurance Companies: Process claims, aid recovery.
  • Chambers of Commerce: Central coordination and updates.

Coordinated Response (MACS)

  • Coordinate with EOCs for permits, access, info.
  • Share intel on workforce, supply chain issues.
  • Join public-private briefings for unified recovery.

Coordinated Actions with Other Groups

Group Coordination Actions
Local Government Provide real-time damage data, resources, attend meetings.
State Government Request support for economic recovery, clean-up assistance.
Federal Government Apply for FEMA & SBA aid, support logistics and security.
